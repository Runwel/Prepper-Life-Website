3 Simple Steps to Create Your Own Mini Survival Kit

Ever wanted to reduce the size of your survival kit but lacked the knowledge or confidence to do so without sacrificing crucial survival supplies?

We know the answer; just make a miniature version of it!

You read that correctly; Itâ€™s all about the mini survival kit you keep in your supply closet, the little survival kit, is the key. It's ideal for situations where you want to go out but yet be prepared without giving others a reason to be suspicious of you.

A survival kit is a bag that contains all the resources you'll need in case of disaster. Survival kits, which are a must-have for preppers and survivalists since they give the tools and resources you need to survive when basic necessities aren't easily accessible, are a typical kit in the armed services.

There are various kinds of survival kits, many of them are designed or created to handle a particular situation. The most typical examples are:

  • Earthquake safety supplies
  • Wilderness emergency supplies
  • Hurricane preparedness tools
  • An automobile emergency kit for the winter.

A Mini Survival Kit: What Is It?

Now, you're probably thinking that everyone ought to be purchasing survival packs like hotcakes. Sure. But you can't exactly send your child to school with a hurricane emergency kit just because you think it would make you feel safer

Mini survival kits are an excellent addition to any prepper's supply because of this. They are the ideal complement to their larger brother, the survival kit, because they are small, compact, concealable, and simple to stow in your luggage.

Survival Kit vs. Mini Survival Kit

Check out the comparison chart below to see how the two compare more clearly:

Survival Kit Mini Survival Kit
Typically, a backpack or duffel bag is used Containers are usually small in size
Can hold more survival items Limited to holding a few survival items only
The items can be curated to a certain emergency situation Has only the general but most important survival items

Now that you are aware of the apparent differences between the two, let's concentrate on the charm of small survival kits.


Why You Should Have a Mini Survival Kit

A small survival kit will do the trick for those unanticipated emergencies that arise out of the blue. You should have one or two of these small kits, but not just for that reason. More examples follow:

Portable

Of course EVERY survival kit is transportable, but what about a tiny kit? portability on a god-tier level. Put it in your luggage or even your pocket and head out on the road with the knowledge that you have a small survival kit with you as a safety net.

Low Profile

A person looking for valuables after SHTF won't be suspicious of a little survival kit next to a duffel bag packed to the brim with provisions. You achieve the preparing aim of being lowkey and reduce your danger of having your belongings taken, just like the gray man hypothesis suggests.

You can create a number of them.

Filling up many survival kits for various eventualities might be taxing on both you and your wallet. Even while we want to be ready for anything, we must also plan wisely. Mini survival kits don't need a lot of supplies, so you can put together a few handfuls to stash in your purse, your car's glove box, or even in the bags of your family. DIY-ing Your Mini Survival Kit in 3 Easy Steps

Even if purchasing a prefabricated micro survival kit is simpler than making one, there are some drawbacks. Cons include

  • The level of quality will depend on your budget.
  • It cannot be customized to meet your needs.
  • The kit's components can be flawed.
  • The list keeps on. So what is the alternative? Of course, DIYing it!

There are lots of do-it-yourself crafts available at Tactical. It's strongly advised to make your own tiny survival kit, especially if you want to choose carefully what goes in it.

Step 1: Choose a Container

The container you choose to utilize is essential for a successful micro survival kit. Since the word "mini" appears in the name, your search must now center on small containers.
It needs to be small and blend in. You have the following choices:

Water Canteen

Although you might not give a water bottle much thought, it works just well for a little survival pack. Choose one with a big enough opening so you can drop your stuff inside or easily remove them.

One reason a metal water bottle is a good choice is that it is more robust. And two, a metal water container will be useful if you ever need to boil water to purify it.


Survival Cases

If tin cans and boxes aren't your style, you can, of course, choose to purchase a survival case. No, a ready-made kitâ€”just the case by itself! Many heavy-duty cases are available for purchase online; some of them are even waterproof and airtight. A tiny survival case is for you if you don't mind spending a few dollars on a high-quality container.


Step 2: Choose Your Items

It's time to decide what to include in your kit once you've decided which container you'll be utilizing.
No, you can't just pick up any random household item that is close by. It involves a little more subtlety than that. You must first take into account the following:
Who is this small emergency pack intended for?
What potential crises could you encounter?
When you understand it, you can pack more strategically. Here are a few items we came up with that are ideal for your little, portable survival kit.


A Notepad and Pencil

going crazy; you'll be OK with a few pieces of paper that have been trimmed out to fit your container and a little pencil. In addition to being used for writing, paper can be used to light a fire, and pencil shavings can be used as tinder.

Money

It's not necessary to use an entire roll of hundreds. You only need a few â‚±100 will do. Avoid adding too much money since you could be tempted to spend it.

Waterproof Machines

Warmth and light are two things that are essential for survival. Starting a fire is one way to get both, therefore it goes without saying that your kit must contain waterproof matches.


Band Aids

A comprehensive medical kit won't fit in your small survival kit. To treat small scrapes and bruises and prevent them from becoming infected, you'll have to make do with a few bandage strips.

Alcohol Wipes

What other medical supplies ought to be included in your kit? Alcoholic cloths. These are sterile pieces of gauze that have been individually wrapped and submerged in alcohol. Particularly if you don't have access to clean water and soap, they are ideal for cleaning wounds.


Sewing Kit

Excellent for patching up holes in clothing, rips in bags, and even closing cuts during life-or-death situations. Naturally, we hope you never need it for that, but it pays to be ready just in case.

Wallet Multitool

What tools are in your small survival kit in addition to a bottle opener and a sharp edge for cutting? It's feasible with a wallet multitool! Wallet multitools were designed to be functional without sacrificing durability or size. Having a wallet multitool in your arsenal is a good idea.



Mini Compass

Let's say an EMP strike occurs when you are in an unfamiliar location. It's unlikely that your phone will be of use in finding your way back home. On the other hand, if you've mastered the underappreciated survival skill of reading maps and compasses, a small compass will be useful!


Step 3: Place in Strategic Areas

After putting everything together, you are presented with a sleek-looking little survival kit. What location are you intending to use?
Though you have a few selections, the greatest locations are typically those that are close at hand. Here are a few illustrations:
In your regularly used bags
Put more than just a small survival kit in your luggage at all times. Add it to the bags you use the most. We're referring to the bags you frequently use, such as your gym bag, your backpack for camping or trekking, and that large, antique suitcase you use when you travel.

In your lockers

You can store your small survival kit in your locker at school, work, or the gym. You spend the most time in these locations, therefore if a calamity were to occur, you would probably be there.

On your desk at work, in a drawer

Again, if you spend the most of your waking hours working, you'll be at your desk or in the office, thus it makes sense to keep a kit in your drawer.

In caches for survival

You might as well put a small survival kit inside if you've set up survival caches along your bug-out path. Get a newly stocked mini survival kit while you're there since a survival cache will let you restock your resources while you're on the run.
